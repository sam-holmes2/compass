# Compass — Journalling Prompts

Optional prompts for going deeper. Upload this file to your Claude project alongside `schema.md` and Claude will use it to ask better questions when you're ready.

You don't need to work through these in order, or all at once. Use them when a session feels flat or you want to go somewhere specific.

---

## Getting oriented

- What's actually on your mind right now — not what you think you should be thinking about?
- What happened this week that you haven't fully processed yet?
- Where are you putting most of your energy? Is that where you'd choose to put it?
- What's the one thing you keep not doing, and what do you think that's about?

---

## Main quest and side quests

- If everything external stayed the same and you could only change one thing about your life, what would it be?
- What does "done" actually look like — specifically, not theoretically?
- What would you be doing if you weren't afraid of it not working?
- Which of your current goals is genuinely yours, and which did you inherit from somewhere else?
- What's the next smallest possible step — the one you could do today?

---

## The four elements

**Air — awareness**
- What has your inner voice been saying most often lately? Is it accurate?
- When do you feel most in your head? What's usually happening just before that?
- What are you avoiding noticing?
- When did you last feel genuinely present — not planning, not reviewing, just here?

**Fire — drive and direction**
- What made you feel most alive this week? What drained you the most?
- Are you doing things because you want to, or because you're supposed to?
- What would you work on if no one would ever know — no audience, no validation?
- Where is your energy going that isn't giving anything back?

**Water — aliveness and desire**
- What do you actually enjoy — not what you think you should enjoy?
- When do you lose track of time? What's different about those moments?
- What have you been denying yourself, and why?
- What does rest actually feel like for you? When did you last have it?

**Earth — beliefs and groundedness**
- What story are you telling yourself about this situation? Is it true?
- Where are you being harder on yourself than the evidence warrants?
- What would you tell a friend who was in your exact position?
- What belief would you have to give up to move forward?

---

## Values and needs

- What matters most to you — not in theory, but based on how you actually spend your time and energy?
- Where do you feel most like yourself? What's present in those moments that's absent in others?
- What do you consistently sacrifice, and is it worth it?
- What need are you currently meeting in ways that cost you?
- When you're at your most difficult, what are you usually not getting enough of?

---

## Relationships and allies

- Who do you feel safest with — where you don't have to manage yourself?
- Who drains you most consistently? What's the pattern?
- Where do you perform rather than just exist?
- Who has seen you clearly — not just the edited version?
- What relationship in your life needs something from you that you haven't given it?

---

## Enemies and patterns

- What pattern keeps showing up across different areas of your life?
- What do you do when you feel threatened or uncertain? What does that protect?
- When do you procrastinate? What are you actually avoiding — failure, success, visibility?
- What's the story you tell yourself to justify staying stuck?
- When did this pattern first appear? What was it protecting against then?

---

## Beliefs

- What do you believe about yourself that you've never questioned?
- Finish this sentence honestly: "People like me don't..."
- What would you need to believe to take the next step?
- What's a belief you hold that the people who know you best would disagree with?
- What evidence are you ignoring because it contradicts the story?

---

## Wins and achievements

- What have you done recently that deserves more credit than you gave it?
- When did you act against your own fear or resistance this week?
- What's something you've improved at that you haven't acknowledged?
- What would past-you be impressed by about where you are now?

---

## End of session

- What's the one thing from this conversation you most want to remember?
- What's the one thing you're going to do differently before the next session?
- What are you still not saying?
